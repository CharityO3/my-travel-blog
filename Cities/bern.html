Bern Travel Guide

Overview

Set along the Aare River and surrounded by hills, Bern offers a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. As Switzerland's capital, it combines the charm of a medieval old town with the relaxed pace of a smaller city.
Beyond its political role, Bern captivates visitors with its UNESCO-listed Altstadt, iconic Zytglogge clock tower, and stunning views from the Rosengarten. The city also boasts excellent museums, like the Zentrum Paul Klee and the Bern Historical Museum.

Stroll under the covered arcades, relax by the river, or hike up the Gurten for panoramic views. Whether you're drawn by its heritage or peaceful atmosphere, Bern offers a quietly impressive Swiss experience.

Flights

As a tourist visiting Bern, your main gateway is likely to be Zurich Airport (ZRH) or Bern Airport (BRN). While Bern Airport handles limited regional flights, most international travelers arrive via Zurichâ€”Switzerland's busiest and best-connected airport. From there, a direct train brings you to Bern in just over an hour.

You can easily compare flights to Zurich or nearby airports like Geneva (GVA) or Basel (BSL) using platforms like Kiwi or Skyscanner. Budget airlines such as easyJet, Vueling, and Eurowings operate frequent routes to these hubs.

Alternatively, if you're traveling from within Europe, consider taking a train or FlixBus for a comfortable and scenic journey directly into Bern's main station, located right in the heart of the city.

Safety

Bern is widely considered a very safe city with low crime rates, a strong focus on public safety and a relaxed, welcoming atmosphere.
While rare, petty theft and pickpocketing can occasionally occurâ€”particularly in busy spots like the main station, popular shopping streets, and public transport hubs.

Stay alert and keep your belongings secure for a worry-free visit.

Budget

Bern, like much of Switzerland, can be on the pricier side compared to other European cities, but with smart planning, it's possible to manage your budget effectively.

Hostel beds typically start around 35-45 Swiss francs, while private hotel rooms begin at around 90-120 francs. Casual meals at local cafÃ©s or restaurants usually range from 15 to 30 francs.
Getting around is simple and efficient thanks to a well-connected network of trams, buses, and regional trains, making it easy to explore both the city and surrounding areas.
