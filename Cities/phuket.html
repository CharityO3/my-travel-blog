Phuket Travel Guide

Overview

Phuket, Thailand's largest island, is a tropical paradise surrounded by the sparkling waters of the Andaman Sea. Its palm-fringed beaches, turquoise bays, and lively coast areas like Patong make it one of Southeast Asia's most popular and picturesque destinations.

Known for its vibrant nightlife, rich culture, and laid-back island vibe, Phuket blends natural beauty with modern comforts. While the island isn't defined by towering mountains, it offers lush jungles, scenic viewpoints, and pristine beaches perfect for relaxation or adventure.

No visit to Phuket is complete without exploring its highlights, from the iconic Big Buddha, Wat Chalong, and Wat Kanan to the colorful streets of Old Phuket Town. Bustling areas like Patong and Kata also offer great food, shopping, and entertainment.

Flights

As a tourist visiting Phuket, your main entry point will be Phuket International Airport (HKT), the island's primary gateway with strong international and domestic connections. It's located about 30-45 minutes drive from popular areas like Patong, Kata, and Phuket Town, depending on traffic.

You can easily find and compare flights to Phuket using platforms like Kiwi or Skyscanner. Many international airlines and regional carriers operate routes to the island, along with budget airlines such as AirAsia and Scoot, making it an accessible destination from across Asia and beyond.
From Europe, Phuket is typically around a 12-hour flight, with airlines like Lufthansa offering connecting routes via Singapore, while Norse provides direct flights from Oslo Airport.

Visitors are required to complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) before entry. Travelers may also be asked to show proof of funds (20,000 THB per person or 40,000 THB per family) upon arrival.

Safety

Phuket is generally considered a safe destination for travelers, with a well-developed tourism infrastructure and a strong local presence of authorities in popular areas.

Violent crime is rare, but as with any busy tourist destination, petty theft and scams can occur especially in crowded areas like Patong Beach, night markets, and busy nightlife zones.

Budget

Phuket is slightly pricier than other parts of Thailand but full of activities. Boat tours to Maya Bay and Phi Phi Island can be booked online via GetYourGuide (~40€), while visiting Instagram spots like the Lilypad (Ma Doo Bua Cafe) (~30€ varying by chosen package), snapping beautiful photos with a Flying Dress at the Rock Beach Swing (~40€ - 30 min Flying Dress + transport), or riding and dancing with elephants at the Lucky Beach Elephant swim (~30€ varying by chosen package), can all be paid for in person, no reservation needed.

Hostel beds start around 8-20 Euros, while private rooms range 32-100 Euros. Street food and casual meals costs around 3-8 Euros. Transport is available via tuk-tuks, songthaews, and taxis, though Grab or Bolt is the fastest and safest option for tourists.
